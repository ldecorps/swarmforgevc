The fix lives in the project's own layer around the vendored parser, per the engineering article's fix-boundary rule.

Now dispatched to coder.

## Process hiccups

- **A stall report that started from a stale coordinator read, then uncovered a real bug.** I had told the operator BL-519 was queued behind BL-515 "with coder still working it" — but by the time the operator checked, coder's inbox was genuinely empty and had been idle for a while. Reconciling against ground truth (pane capture, mailbox files, git logs, not memory) found two real, independent causes stacked on top of each other: (1) coder's forward of the BL-515 bounce-fix to cleaner had its tmux wake-up fail (`tmux send-literal failed`) — expected under mono-router since cleaner has no standing pane, but the resident never followed up by rotating into cleaner to collect its own forwarded work; (2) cleaner's queue was separately blocked by a **stale, three-hour-old in-process batch record** from earlier in the session — work that had, in fact, already been forwarded downstream and merged by architect, just never marked complete locally. Verified via git ancestry and architect's own completed inbox before touching anything, closed the phantom batch record (pure mailbox bookkeeping, not domain work), and sent the resident an explicit nudge to rotate rather than attempting to manipulate its live tmux pane directly from a separate session.
- **A QA bounce landed on a ticket already moved to `backlog/done/`.** BL-515's first QA approval (`8e540eb9a6`) was closed out per the normal bookkeeping flow at 04:56 — then QA's own further review caught a documenter-forward defect (dropped hardener lineage) and bounced it anyway, independent of the ticket-tracking state. The ticket record itself was never wrong (BL-490 saw an identical pattern yesterday: a ticket's `done/` record staying correct while its underlying code needed one more round through the pipeline) — but it's worth flagging as a recurring shape: ticket-closed and code-fully-landed are not always the same moment under a fast bounce cycle, and today's mono-router rotation makes that gap more visible than the old standing-fleet pack did.
- **One dropped handoff (mechanical), not a defect:** the coder→cleaner forward of the BL-515 fix landed correctly in cleaner's inbox despite the tmux wake failing — the daemon's copy-then-wake sequence degraded safely (content delivered, only the notification failed), exactly as the corrupt/failed-handoff quarantine machinery is designed to do. No parcel was lost; the gap was purely "nobody was told to look."
